Podcast Discussion 2

S. Town Episode

Source: express.co.uk

John B McLemore's Maze

Structure of S. Town Episode

The structure of this podcast falls in a story form that is mostly exploratory in nature. The main host explores a central character-John B. McLemore-and his setting-"Shit Town", Alabama.

Who Does the Talking?

Most of the talking is done by Brian Reed, the host, and John B., captured through recorded conversations.

Is it Scripted?

Brian's narration could be a bit scripted but it flows very well. The informal conversations with John B. are definitely not scripted but Brian could have prepared questions for John B. before their meetings.

Interviews?

This episode is primarily composed of informal interviews between Brian and John B., interspersed with Brian's narration of the story.

Sound Effects and Music?

Sound effects and music were used quite a bit throughout this show. They were:

  • Plucky violin music (played at beginning)

  • Light ambient music in the background (embellishes certain parts of the story)

  • Ends with the song "A Rose for Emily" by the Zombies because of its connection to the Faulkner book mentioned in the story

Serial Episode

Source: wordpress

Serial's Adnan Syed

Structure of Serial Episode

The structure of this podcast episode is set in a story form, told primarily by the main host and interspersed with live recordings that help move the story along. This is a crime podcast that is also very character-driven; new characters integral to the story are always introduced.

Who Does the Talking?

Most of the talking is done by the show's host, Sarah Koenig. But many times you will hear snippets of voices of other characters that are part of the story.

Is it Scripted?

Perhaps Sarah Koenig's speaking points are a bit scripted, but the way in which she delivers them is very natural and makes the story flow well.

Interviews?

Apart from Sarah's storytelling, the majority of the episode is made up of formal interrogations that were previously recorded and informal interviews with characters that drive this story.

Sound Effects and Music?

The main sound sequence that pops up is a mysterious, tip-toeing type melody that repeats at the beginning, end, and sometimes throughout the episode.
